Foggintor Quarry

  • Foggintor was originally a tor - a typical hill on Dartmoor.
  • Foggintor Quarry opened in the early 1800's, with a few outbuildings to house workers and process the granite.
  • There is speculation, but insuffcient evidence, that stone from Fogggintor was used in London Bridge. Granite from here was definitely used in Nelson's Column and Dartmoor Prison, however.
  • The site was closed down in 1906, due to rival quarries at Haytor and having better transport links to London.
  • For more information on the geology of the area, see the Variscan Coast website.
  • The area is now a popular place to visit for climbers, walkers, cyclists and the odd wild swimmer.
  • To walk there, you can approach from the North, at the car park grid reference 750568 on the B3357; from Princetown along the Granite Way cycle path, or from the South West, parking near Eggworthy, at 723550.
